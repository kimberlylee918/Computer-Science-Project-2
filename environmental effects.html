Homeless people tend to release biohazardous materials such as blood, vomit, and other bodily fluids.


The release of human waste in this manner contaminates the ground and water. It also exposes residents of encampments and other members of the public to hazardous materials.


On a more general basis, homeless encampments cause broad damage to wilderness areas and parks.
