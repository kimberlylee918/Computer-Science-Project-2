What is Homelessness?

Homelessness is "the situation of an individual, family, or community without stable, safe, permanent, appropriate housing, or the immediate prospect means and ability of acquiring it."


What is Poverty?

The state of being extremely poor.


How many Elders are in poverty in South Korea?

In South Korea, almost half of its elderly population over the age of 65 live in poverty, according to a 2016 OECD economic survey. About a quarter live alone. Many grapple with living in isolation and depression.


How many people are homeless in South Korea?

2,741 were male and 732 were female along with five being gender-unknown. Of the total, 731 lived on the streets and 2,747 stayed in temporary shelters.


What age does homelessness start for their people?

Homeless people in Seoul are 54.6 years old on average, and they started living on the streets or in shelters at 42.3, according to Seoul City, Thursday.
